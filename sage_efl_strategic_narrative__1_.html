Campaign overview

Giving "90 Minutes Back" to EFL fans

This campaign ran from 9 February to 16 March 2026, building on Sage giving "90 Minutes Back" to EFL fans across the country. Unlike previous campaigns, this was targeted at the mass EFL football fan as well as small business owners.
Fans entered for the chance to win a behind-the-scenes experience at their local EFL club, including matchday tickets and a replica EFL match ball.
Paid promotion was pushed out across Sage's channels whilst organic promotion went out across the EFL and all 72 EFL clubs' channels.
Channels used
Paid — Sage channels
Sage CRM (Eloqua), paid social (Facebook), paid search (Google, Bing), Sage website
Organic — EFL & club channels
EFL league-wide email distribution, all 72 EFL club email databases, club organic social, EFL organic social
What was different this time
Previous competitions targeted small business owners specifically. This activation deliberately broadened the audience to the mass EFL fan base, using the prize mechanic as a universal entry point.
The data review that follows examines who entered, which channels drove them, and where Sage's target audience sits within the broader mix.

Email is the dominant channel but splits sharply by source. Club database sends drove 5,085 entries at a 20% business owner rate. Eloqua (Sage's CRM) delivered just 665 entries but at a 49% owner rate — nearly half of Sage-sourced entrants run a business.
Social media (Facebook + Instagram) accounts for 26%, with a 22% business owner rate on Facebook and 16% on Instagram. Social fills the top of the funnel effectively.
CPC (Google + Bing) delivered just 37 entries but both channels show 60%+ business owner rates. The sample is small — but the quality signal is consistent across both search engines.
Strategic implications

Two channel stories

1. Paid search is the efficiency opportunity
Google and Bing together delivered just 37 entries, but over 30% were financial decision makers. The sample is small and the rates may not hold at scale. But the signal is strong enough to invest in a dedicated test: paid search against finance and accounting audiences, targeting the competition landing page, at 10x the current spend.
2. Club emails and social are the reach engine
5,358 entries from club database sends at a 3.4% decision maker rate. Facebook contributed 2,101 entries at 3.8%. These channels generate the headline entry volume and broad brand exposure, but are not where Sage's core prospects concentrate. Each channel should be measured against role-appropriate KPIs — not a single blended metric.
Data quality

Opportunities to strengthen future campaigns

The dataset is rich but has gaps that, if addressed, would significantly improve lead quality and attribution clarity.
No post-entry conversion tracking. The data captures who entered but not who progressed to email opens, website visits, demo requests or purchases. Connecting competition data to downstream conversion would allow ROI measurement beyond lead capture.
Industry is inferred, not captured. With 3,973 unique free-text job titles, industry classification is directional. A structured industry dropdown on the competition form would deliver clean segmentation.

Summary

What the data proves

The EFL audience contains Sage's commercial audience
24.4% of competition entrants are business owners. The sponsorship is reaching business-owning football fans — not just football fans. That audience overlap is the foundation of the partnership's commercial value, and 10,235 entries prove it is not anecdotal.
National reach and ticket allocation
The EFL-owned audience is an effective way to engage fans across the country. It provides a potential opportunity to give away tickets in volume in the future, extending the partnership's value beyond digital activations and into matchday experiences.
EFL audience
Effective in driving volume into competitions. EFL solus emails remain the most valuable asset and will continue to do so in any digital activation going forward.
